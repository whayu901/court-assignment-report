# Court Reporting Industry Research & Benchmarks

Research findings to add realistic context and features to the workflow system.

## Industry Overview (2024)

### Market Trends
- **Digital Transformation**: Industry shifting from traditional stenography to digital/AI-assisted reporting
- **Stenographer Shortage**: 42% drop in stenography schools, 74% drop in enrollment
- **Demographics**: 81% of stenographers are 45+ years old
- **Cost Savings**: Digital reporting offers 40-50% savings vs traditional stenography
- **Speed Priority**: 30% of clients cite faster delivery as primary reason for choosing digital reporters

### Accuracy Standards
- **Industry Standard**: 99%+ accuracy required for legal documentation
- **Modern Expectation**: Clients expect near 100% accuracy
- **Method**: AI transcription + human review/editing
- **Certification**: American Association of Electronic Reporters and Transcribers

## Types of Legal Proceedings

### Deposition Types

#### By Format
1. **Oral Deposition** (Most Common)
   - In-person Q&A under oath
   - Court reporter transcribes
   - Typically 3-7 hours duration

2. **Written Deposition**
   - Pre-submitted questions
   - Written responses
   - Used for simple/uncontested matters

3. **Video Deposition**
   - Recorded on video + transcript
   - Used for key witnesses
   - Higher cost but preserves demeanor

4. **Telephonic/Remote Deposition**
   - Conducted via phone/video conference
   - Increased usage post-COVID
   - Lower cost, no travel

#### By Location
1. **In-Person**: Law office or court
2. **Remote**: All participants in different locations
3. **Hybrid**: Mix of in-person and remote participants

#### By Purpose
1. **Discovery Deposition**: Gather information pre-trial
2. **Evidence Deposition**: Preserve testimony for trial
3. **Expert Witness Deposition**: Technical/professional opinions
4. **Character Witness Deposition**: Character testimony

### Case Types

#### Civil Cases
- **Personal Injury**: Car accidents, medical malpractice, slip-and-fall
- **Commercial Disputes**: Contract disputes, business litigation
- **Employment**: Discrimination, wrongful termination, harassment
- **Family Law**: Divorce, custody, support
- **Real Estate**: Property disputes, construction defects
- **Intellectual Property**: Patent, trademark, copyright

#### Criminal Cases
- **Limited Use**: Depositions less common in criminal
- **Exceptional Circumstances**: Preserve testimony if witness unavailable
- **Court Approval Required**: Judge must grant motion

#### Administrative Hearings
- **Workers Compensation**
- **Social Security Disability**
- **Professional License Disputes**
- **Regulatory Proceedings**

## Workflow Stages in Reality

### Complete Production Pipeline

1. **Pre-Deposition** (1-3 days before)
   - Schedule reporter/videographer
   - Confirm location and equipment
   - Review special instructions
   - Prepare exhibits

2. **Deposition Day**
   - Reporter arrival (15-30 min early)
   - Equipment setup and testing
   - Witness oath administration
   - Real-time transcription (if ordered)
   - Exhibit marking and handling
   - Duration: Average 3-5 hours

3. **Post-Deposition Processing**
   - Audio file backup and storage
   - Rough draft production (if ordered)
   - Full transcription
   - Quality assurance review
   - Exhibit scanning and indexing

4. **Review & Correction** (Optional)
   - Witness review period: 30 days
   - Attorney review
   - Corrections and errata sheet
   - Final certification

5. **Final Delivery**
   - Certified transcript production
   - Digital and/or paper copies
   - Exhibit bundles
   - Audio/video files
   - Keyword index (if ordered)

### Quality Control Checkpoints
- ✅ Audio quality verification
- ✅ Spelling verification (names, technical terms)
- ✅ Timestamp accuracy
- ✅ Exhibit reference accuracy
- ✅ Format compliance
- ✅ Final proofread
